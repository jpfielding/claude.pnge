# Key Formations for Lithium and Magnesium in Produced Waters

Reference table of U.S. formations with significant lithium and/or magnesium
concentrations in produced waters, based on the USGS National Produced Waters
Geochemical Database v3.0 and published literature.

---

## Lithium-Rich Formations

Economic threshold for Direct Lithium Extraction (DLE): approximately **100-150 mg/L Li**
depending on technology and brine volume. Some emerging DLE technologies target
concentrations as low as 50 mg/L.

| Formation | Basin | States | Li Range (mg/L) | Typical TDS (mg/L) | PLAYTYPE | Notes |
|-----------|-------|--------|-----------------|---------------------|----------|-------|
| Smackover | Gulf Coast | AR, TX, LA, MS | 50-477 | 150,000-350,000 | Sedimentary | Highest known U.S. brine Li; Standard Lithium project in AR |
| Marcellus | Appalachian | WV, PA, OH, NY | 10-200+ | 50,000-350,000 | Shale | Large flowback/produced water volumes; Li correlates with TDS |
| Utica / Point Pleasant | Appalachian | OH, WV, PA | 20-150 | 100,000-300,000 | Shale | Often co-produced with Marcellus; slightly deeper |
| Oriskany | Appalachian | WV, PA, NY | 10-100 | 50,000-250,000 | Sedimentary | Devonian sandstone; variable Li |
| Bakken | Williston | ND, MT | 10-70 | 100,000-350,000 | Shale | Large water volumes; moderate Li |
| Wolfcamp | Permian | TX, NM | 5-50 | 30,000-250,000 | Shale | Massive production volumes; low-moderate Li |
| Eagle Ford | Gulf Coast | TX | 5-30 | 20,000-150,000 | Shale | High water production; generally lower Li |
| Devonian Shale | Appalachian | WV, PA, OH, KY | 5-50 | 20,000-200,000 | Shale | Older production; variable chemistry |
| Clinton | Appalachian | OH, WV, PA | 5-40 | 50,000-300,000 | Sedimentary | Silurian sandstone |
| Tuscaloosa | Gulf Coast | LA, MS | 10-80 | 50,000-250,000 | Sedimentary | Deep Cretaceous sands |

## Magnesium-Rich Formations

Magnesium is generally abundant in produced waters. Typical oilfield brines
contain 500-5,000 mg/L Mg. Recovery is potentially economic from brines with
very high TDS where Mg exceeds approximately 2,000-3,000 mg/L, especially
when co-produced with lithium.

| Formation | Basin | States | Mg Range (mg/L) | Typical TDS (mg/L) | Notes |
|-----------|-------|--------|-----------------|---------------------|-------|
| Smackover | Gulf Coast | AR, TX, LA | 1,000-5,000+ | 150,000-350,000 | Highest Mg in some AR brines |
| Marcellus | Appalachian | WV, PA, OH | 500-3,000 | 50,000-350,000 | Mg increases with well age and TDS |
| Bakken | Williston | ND, MT | 500-4,000 | 100,000-350,000 | High Mg in many Bakken brines |
| Utica | Appalachian | OH, WV, PA | 500-2,500 | 100,000-300,000 | Similar Mg profile to Marcellus |
| Permian Basin (various) | Permian | TX, NM | 300-3,000 | 30,000-300,000 | Multiple productive intervals |
| Michigan Basin (various) | Michigan | MI | 1,000-5,000 | 200,000-400,000 | Some of the saltiest U.S. brines |
| Williston Basin (various) | Williston | ND, MT | 500-4,000 | 100,000-400,000 | Multiple intervals with high Mg |

## Li/Mg Co-Recovery Targets

The most promising formations for combined Li + Mg recovery are those with:
1. Li concentration above approximately 100 mg/L (DLE-economic)
2. Mg concentration above approximately 2,000 mg/L
3. High, sustained produced water volumes
4. Existing gathering and disposal infrastructure

**Top candidates:**
- **Smackover (AR)** -- highest Li (up to 477 mg/L) with high Mg (1,000-5,000 mg/L)
- **Marcellus (WV/PA)** -- Li 50-200+ mg/L in mature wells, Mg 500-3,000 mg/L, massive water volumes
- **Utica (OH)** -- Li 50-150 mg/L, Mg 500-2,500 mg/L, growing production
